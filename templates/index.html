Soil pH and nutrient values are critical factors in determining the quality and productivity of farming. The pH of the soil determines the availability of nutrients to plants, with most crops thriving in slightly acidic to neutral soils with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5. Nutrients such as nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K) are essential for plant growth and development, and their availability in the soil can greatly impact crop yield and quality.

Generally, a soil test is conducted to determine the NPK values, and based on the results, farmers can determine the optimal amount of fertilizers to apply to their fields to maintain a healthy and productive soil. Proper management of soil pH and NPK values is crucial for sustainable and profitable farming.
